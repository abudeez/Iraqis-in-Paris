Masgouf

✻

Carp fish1

lemon4


The fish is caught alive and weighed. It is clubbed to kill it, partially scaled and gutted. The fish is then split lengthwise down the back, washed and spread out into a single flat piece. This opens the fish into the shape of a large, symmetrical circle, while leaving the belly intact. From there, the cook bastes the inside of the fish with a marinade of olive oil, rock salt, tamarind, and ground turmeric. Crushed tomatoes and coriander are sometimes added to the marinade. The fish is then either impaled on two sharp iron spikes, or placed in a big iron, clamshell grill with a handle and a locking snare, designed specifically for this dish.

The fish, either clamped in the grill or mounted on the spikes, is then placed next to the fire on the "fire altar", a feature shared by all masgûf restaurants. This "altar" typically consists of a big open-air area centered by a raised, podium-like sandbox that is either round, octagonal or sometimes rectangular, in the middle of which there is a large fire of apricot tree logs.

Chicken Machboos

✻

A classic chicken and rice.

Chicken Machboos is a traditional Iraqi rice dish that is known for its unique flavor and aroma.

The dish is made by cooking chicken in a blend of spices, including saffron, turmeric, and cinnamon, then serving it on a bed of flavorful rice.

The dish is often garnished with fried onions and served with a side of spicy tomato sauce. It is a popular dish in the Middle East and is enjoyed by many for its delicious taste and hearty texture.

Dolma

✻

Stuffed vegetables with meat and rice

Iraqi dolma is a delicious dish made of stuffed vegetables, typically tomatoes, peppers, and onions..

The filling is a mix of rice, ground meat, and spices,Dolma is a staple food in Iraqi cuisine and is often served with a side of yogurt or a tomato-based sauce

It's a dish that's both satisfying and filling, making it perfect for a family meal or special occasion. .

Lamb Kebabs

✻

Lamb kebabs are a popular dish in Middle Eastern cuisine, particularly in Iraq.

The dish involves skewering cubes of marinated lamb and grilling them to perfection.

The lamb is typically marinated in a blend of spices like cumin, coriander, and paprika, giving it a rich flavor.

The charred edges and tender texture of the meat create a beautiful balance of taste and texture, making it a popular and mouth-watering dish.
